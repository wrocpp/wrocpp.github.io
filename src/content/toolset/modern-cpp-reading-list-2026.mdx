This page is the curated, quarterly-refreshed reference for the modern + secure + safe-by-default C++ conversation as it stands in May 2026. The companion piece is the essay [**C++ Safety State of the Union: May 2026**](/posts/cpp-safety-state-of-the-union-may-2026/) -- read that for narrative; come back here when you need to dig into a specific source.

Editorial bias: implementation-grade sources only. Committee papers, first-hand industry deployment reports, named expert bloggers. No hot-take aggregators.

## The committee's papers

WG21 publishes papers in monthly mailings; the May 2026 mailing dropped early this month (116 papers, pre-Brno). The papers below are the load-bearing ones for the safety story:

- **[P2900R14 Contracts](https://www.open-std.org/jtc1/sc22/wg21/docs/papers/2025/p2900r14.pdf)** (Berne, Doumler, Krzemienski et al.) -- the C++26 contracts proposal as adopted. Four evaluation semantics, replaceable violation handler, per-TU granularity. Contested vote (114-12-3) at Croydon.
- **[P3471R4 Hardened standard library](https://www.open-std.org/jtc1/sc22/wg21/docs/papers/2025/p3471r4.html)** (Varlamov, Dionne) -- the first standard library consumer of Contracts. Includes the Google deployment data (0.3% perf cost / 1000+ bugs).
- **[P2996R12 Reflection](https://www.open-std.org/jtc1/sc22/wg21/docs/papers/2024/p2996r12.pdf)** (Childers, Sutton, Revzin, Yang et al.) -- the foundation for user-library safety patterns.
- **[P3970R0 Profiles and Safety: a call to action](https://www.open-std.org/jtc1/sc22/wg21/docs/papers/2026/p3970r0.pdf)** (Vandevoorde, Garland, McKenney, Orr, Stroustrup, Wong; Jan 2026) -- the timeline-defending paper. Names three milestones to keep profiles on track for C++29.
- **[D3984R0 A type-safety profile](https://www.stroustrup.com/type-safety-profile.pdf)** (Stroustrup; Jan 2026) -- the concrete proposal that sits on top of [P3589R1 Profiles framework](https://isocpp.org/files/papers/P3589R1.pdf) (Dos Reis).
- **[P5000R1 Direction for ISO C++29](https://www.open-std.org/jtc1/sc22/wg21/docs/papers/2026/p5000r1.pdf)** (Vandevoorde, Garland, McKenney, Orr, Stroustrup, Wong; R0 Feb 2026, revised May 2026) -- the formal direction-setting paper for the next standard. Safety is the named headline axis.
- **[P3543R0 Response to P3081](https://www.open-std.org/jtc1/sc22/wg21/docs/papers/2024/p3543r0.pdf)** (Gill, Jabot, Lakos, Berne, Doumler) -- the Bloomberg-led counter-paper that contributed to the C++26 profile deferral. Argues granularity, error reporting, and migration paths need rework.
- **[P3294 Token sequence injection](https://www.open-std.org/jtc1/sc22/wg21/docs/papers/2024/p3294r3.pdf)** (Revzin, Alexandrescu, Vandevoorde) -- the C++29 candidate that wro.cpp's "Where this is heading" triptych sections all gesture at. Re-revised in the May 2026 mailing.

## The talks worth your hour

Five videos that frame the 2026 conversation:

- **["Reflection Is Only Half the Story"](https://schedule.cppnow.org/session/2026/reflection-is-only-half-the-story/)** -- Barry Revzin, C++Now 2026 keynote (4 May, Aspen). The tour of source-code-generation design space across Rust, Swift, D. Video drops ~mid-June; until then the wro.cpp coverage at <PostLink slug="revzin-reflection-is-only-half">revzin-reflection-is-only-half</PostLink> is the best secondary.
- **["Concept-based Generic Programming"](https://isocpp.org/blog/2026/05/cppcon-2025-concept-based-generic-programming-bjarne-stroustrup1)** -- Bjarne Stroustrup, CppCon 2025 keynote (re-shared on isocpp.org May 2026). The mechanism C++ has had since C++20 for safe user-extension of the type system. Pair-reads well with the reflection arc.
- **"C++: Growing in a world of competition, safety, and AI"** -- Herb Sutter, BeCPP Symposium 2026 (March 30, Howest). The SlashData developer-growth data + the regulatory-pressure framing in 60 minutes. Local coverage: <PostLink slug="cpp-fastest-growing-2025">cpp-fastest-growing-2025</PostLink>.
- **"Making C++ Standard Parallelism Multidimensional"** -- Mark Hoemmen, C++Now 2026 (May 6). `std::execution` extended to multidimensional parallelism. Touches the simd-in-cpp-2026 toolset entry.
- **"Benchmarking - It's About Time"** -- Matt Godbolt, C++Now 2026 (May 8). Performance measurement done right. Touches the profiling-cpp-2026 toolset entry.

## The industry deployment reports

What the people who ship C++ at scale actually wrote down:

- **[Chromium memory safety page](https://www.chromium.org/Home/chromium-security/memory-safety/)** -- the canonical first-hand account. 70% of high-severity bugs are memory bugs; library hardening + LLVM plugins + presubmit checks + MiraclePtr; sandboxing economics.
- **[Google "Retrofits Spatial Memory Safety Onto C++"](https://thenewstack.io/google-retrofits-spatial-memory-safety-onto-c/)** (The New Stack) -- MiraclePtr cut use-after-free bugs by 57%. V8 heap sandbox beta. Project Naptime. The numbers the rest of the industry quotes.
- **[Android memory safety docs](https://source.android.com/docs/security/test/memory-safety)** -- > 60% of high-severity vulnerabilities are memory bugs; Android 12+ ships systematic mitigations; MTE adoption status.
- **["How Google Is Improving C++ Memory Safety"](https://sos-vo.org/news/programming-languages-how-google-improving-c-memory-safety)** -- Chandler Carruth's attribution of the LLVM-side tipping point to Apple's libc++ safe-buffers work. The "we no longer have to make painful tradeoffs of performance or security" line.
- **[C++ Trends 2026](https://slackism.com/c-trends-2026/)** (Slackism) -- the only single-page survey of the rollout state across vendors as of early 2026. Useful as an index even if you read it skeptically.

## The blog posts

Named bloggers writing through 2026 -- the ones who've earned the editorial trust to be worth your hour:

- **["Legacy Safety: The Wroclaw C++ Meeting"](https://cor3ntin.github.io/posts/profiles/)** (cor3ntin) -- the best community-side write-up of how the committee worked through the profiles-vs-Safe-C++ question. The Wroclaw venue is why this reading list exists.
- **["What reinterpret_cast doesn't do"](https://isocpp.org/blog/2026/05/what-reinterpret-cast-doesnt-do-andreas-fertig)** (Andreas Fertig, 2026-05-18). Plus the sibling [`std::launder`](https://andreasfertig.com/blog/) post (2026-05-05) and the noexcept-move post (2026-05-20). The object-lifetime trilogy. Local coverage: <PostLink slug="fertig-reinterpret-cast">fertig-reinterpret-cast</PostLink>.
- **["Contracts in C++26. Four Practical Use Cases"](https://starsurgeon.medium.com/contracts-in-c-26-3916457f0ca2)** (Andreas Müller, April 2026) -- the most usable production playbook for P2900 contracts I've found.
- **["Practical Security in Production"](https://queue.acm.org/detail.cfm?id=3773097)** (ACM Queue) -- the sanitizer-matrix mindset, fuzzing as invariant-oracle, contracts-as-fuzz-target.
- **["Safe C++ proposal flames out"](https://www.infoworld.com/article/4065702/safe-c-proposal-for-memory-safety-flames-out.html)** (InfoWorld) and **["Safe C++ all but abandoned"](https://www.theregister.com/2025/09/16/safe_c_proposal_ditched/)** (The Register) -- the press coverage of the Hagenberg vote outcome. Useful for the external-narrative framing of the technical decision.

## The regulatory references

The policy framing without which the technical conversation makes no sense:

- **[CISA Memory Safe Languages directive](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/news/case-memory-safe-roadmaps)** -- the Jan 2026 deadline for manufacturer memory-safety roadmaps. The directive itself is short; the [CISA + NSA + FBI joint guidance](https://media.defense.gov/2023/Dec/06/2003352724/-1/-1/0/THE-CASE-FOR-MEMORY-SAFE-ROADMAPS-TLP-CLEAR.PDF) fills out the framing.
- **[EU Cyber Resilience Act (CRA)](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/cyber-resilience-act)** -- comparable obligations for software placed on the EU market.
- **["C++ creator calls for action to address 'serious attacks'"](https://www.theregister.com/2025/03/02/c_creator_calls_for_action/)** (The Register, March 2025) -- the public-facing report of Stroustrup's "credible threat" framing.
- **["C++ founder champions profiles for memory safety"](https://www.infoworld.com/article/3839386/c-plus-plus-founder-champions-profiles-for-memory-safety.html)** (InfoWorld, March 2025) -- the same framing with more committee-process detail.
- **[Stroustrup's February 2025 note to WG21](https://www.stroustrup.com/WG21.html)** -- the primary source for the "unprecedented, serious attacks on C++" language. His full papers list at the same URL.
