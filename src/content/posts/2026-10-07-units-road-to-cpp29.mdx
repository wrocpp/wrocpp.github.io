Every demo in this series ran on a third-party library. The finale is about the plan to make that library unnecessary.

**P3045, "Quantities and units library,"** is the WG21 proposal to bring what you have seen — quantities with units in their types, kind safety, the affine space, formatting — into the C++ standard library itself, targeting **C++29**. mp-units is its reference implementation, which means everything this series demonstrated is, in effect, a preview build of a possible `std` feature. The idea has been cooking in the committee since P1935 first floated it in 2019; the current effort is the most complete units-library design ever put in front of WG21.

## What the signature buys you

The core of the pitch fits in one function:

<GodboltEmbed id="bzef1hnrx" gccOnly title="Interfaces that say what they take, not just which type" gcc_subtitle="x86-64 GCC 16.1 · -std=c++23 -O2 · mp-units trunk · runs live" />

`avg_speed(quantity<si::metre>, quantity<si::second>)` is a signature that carries the physics. Callers may pass kilometres or hours — the conversions are exact and automatic — but *swapping the arguments does not compile*. Compare that with the `double avg_speed(double, double)` every codebase has today, where the only defense is parameter naming and hope. Multiply by every interface in a flight controller, a medical device, a trading system, and that is the case for standardization: safety infrastructure this fundamental should not depend on which third-party library a project happened to adopt.

## The Polish story

For this publication, the finale has a second layer. P3045's lead author is **Mateusz Pusz**: a principal C++ architect from Gdańsk, a voting member of the ISO C++ committee since 2017, contributor to MISRA C++, author of accepted C++20 papers (heterogeneous lookup for unordered containers, P0919/P0920), and co-founder of the PKN subcommittee through which Poland became a P-member of the ISO C++ committee — meaning Poland votes on what C++ becomes.

The strongest push to give C++ a standard language of physical quantities is being led from Poland, in the open, on GitHub, with a decade of library iterations behind it. Standardization is slow and nothing is guaranteed until the final plenary vote — but if P3045 lands, code like this series' demos will need nothing but `import std;`.

Until then: the library is a `find_package(mp-units)` away, every episode's demo runs live in your browser, and the whole series is linked below.
