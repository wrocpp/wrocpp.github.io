Meeting C++, the main European C++ conference, returns to Berlin from **26 to 28 November 2026**, hybrid for the fifth year so you can attend onsite or online. Two of the three keynotes are announced.

The first is **Mateusz Pusz**, author of [mp-units](/posts/units-road-to-cpp29/), the physical-quantities-and-units library that drew unanimous support in its first LEWG review this spring and is now working its way toward C++29. We have spent a six-part series on what that library does and why dimensional analysis in the type system is worth the trouble, so the author giving a conference keynote is a fitting bookend. Expect the talk to cover where the standardization effort stands and what the library looks like now.

The second announced keynote is **Kate Gregory**, a reliably excellent speaker on the human side of the craft, the design decisions and team dynamics that shape how C++ actually gets written. The third keynote slot is still open.

If you follow one European C++ conference, this is usually the one, and the program tends to fill out well before the autumn. Worth putting on the calendar now.
