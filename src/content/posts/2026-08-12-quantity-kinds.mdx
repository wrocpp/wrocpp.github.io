Classic dimensional analysis, the kind every physics student learns and most units libraries implement, has a blind spot: it only sees the *dimension* of a quantity, not its *meaning*.

Consider three quantities: the refresh rate of a display (144 Hz), the activity of a radioactive source (144 Bq), and an angular velocity (144 rad/s). Dimensionally they are all the same thing: **1/s**. A library that checks dimensions and nothing else will let you add all three together and hand you a well-typed, physically meaningless answer.

The hertz and the becquerel exist as separate SI units precisely *because* the meanings differ — the SI brochure is explicit that Hz is for periodic phenomena and Bq for stochastic radioactive decay, and that they must not be interchanged. Your compiler was never told.

## Kinds: what mp-units checks that dimensions cannot

mp-units pioneered **quantity kind** safety: every quantity belongs to a kind in the ISO 80000 taxonomy, and two quantities with the same dimension but different kinds do not mix.

The two commented lines are the point. `refresh_rate + source_decay` is rejected — not because the dimensions disagree (they do not), but because *frequency* and *activity* are different kinds of quantity. Likewise you cannot initialize a becquerel quantity from a hertz one. Meanwhile everything physics does permit still works: inverting the refresh rate gives a perfectly good period, converted to milliseconds with `.in(ms)`.

This is the feature that separates mp-units from a decade of "typed double" libraries, and it comes straight from modeling the **International System of Quantities** rather than just its units. Energy and torque both come out as newton-metres; frequency and activity both come out as 1/s; a dimensions-only checker shrugs at both confusions. A kinds-aware one catches them at compile time.

If the <PostLink slug="unit-bug-mars-orbiter">Mars Climate Orbiter bug</PostLink> was the easy case — wrong unit, same quantity — this is the subtle one: right dimension, wrong quantity. Both end the same way without a type system that knows physics.
