Every demo in this series ran on a third-party library. The finale is about the plan to make that library unnecessary.

**P3045, "Quantities and units library,"** is the WG21 proposal to move what you have seen into the C++ standard library itself, targeting **C++29**: quantities with units in their types, kind safety, the affine space, formatting. mp-units is its reference implementation, so everything this series demonstrated is, in effect, a preview build of a possible `std` feature. The idea has been in front of the committee since P1935 first floated it in 2019, and P3045 is where it stands now.

## The signature carries the physics

The core of the pitch fits in one function:

<GodboltEmbed id="bzef1hnrx" gccOnly title="Interfaces that say what they take, not just which type" gcc_subtitle="x86-64 GCC 16.1 · -std=c++23 -O2 · mp-units trunk · runs live" />

`avg_speed(quantity<si::metre>, quantity<si::second>)` is a signature that carries the physics. Callers may pass kilometres or hours, and the conversions are exact and automatic, but *swapping the arguments does not compile*. Compare that with the `double avg_speed(double, double)` every codebase has today, where the only defense is parameter naming and hope. Multiply that by every interface in a flight controller, a medical device, a trading system, and you have the case for standardization: safety this fundamental should not depend on which third-party library a project happened to adopt.

## P3045's author and Poland's committee seat

This finale has a local angle too. P3045's lead author is **Mateusz Pusz**, a principal C++ architect from Gdańsk. He has been a voting member of the ISO C++ committee since 2017, contributes to MISRA C++, and wrote accepted C++20 papers (heterogeneous lookup for unordered containers, P0919/P0920). He also co-founded the PKN subcommittee through which Poland became a P-member of the ISO C++ committee, which means Poland votes on what C++ becomes.

So the work toward a standard vocabulary for physical quantities is happening in the open, on GitHub, with a decade of library iterations behind it. Standardization is slow, and nothing is guaranteed until the final plenary vote. If P3045 lands, code like this series' demos will need nothing but `import std;`.

Until then, the library is a `find_package(mp-units)` away, every episode's demo is runnable in the Godbolt embeds, and the whole series is linked below.
