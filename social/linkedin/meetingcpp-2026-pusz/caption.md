# Meeting C++ 2026 lands in Berlin with a units-library keynote

## Body
Meeting C++, the main European C++ conference, returns to Berlin from 26 to 28 November 2026, hybrid for the fifth year so you can attend onsite or online. Two of the three keynotes are announced.

The first is Mateusz Pusz, author of mp-units, the physical-quantities-and-units library that drew unanimous support in its first LEWG review this spring and is now working its way toward C++29. We spent a six-part series on what that library does and why dimensional analysis in the type system earns its keep, so the author giving a keynote is a fitting bookend.

The second is Kate Gregory, a reliably excellent speaker on the human side of the craft, the design decisions and team dynamics that shape how C++ actually gets written. The third slot is still open.

If you follow one European C++ conference, this is usually the one, and the program fills out well before autumn: https://wrocpp.github.io/posts/meetingcpp-2026-pusz/
